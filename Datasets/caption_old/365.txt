The FPPI of Canadian fresh fruit in 2016 fluctuates steadily between January and April. Then the FPPI start to decline, with its downwards slope increasing slightly after May. The FPPI drops dramatically in July, and reaches its lowest point in August. It then quickly starts raising, slowing slightly between September and October, and finishes its climb in November. The FPPI is close to its January value and is fairly steady until December.
